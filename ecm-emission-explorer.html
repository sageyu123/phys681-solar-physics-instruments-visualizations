ECM Explorer

In a high-B, low-ne plasma, the electron distribution can carry free energy in the perpendicular direction (a loss cone or horseshoe). Where ∂f/∂v⊥ > 0 and the ratio ωpe/Ωce ≲ 1, the resonance condition ω − k∥ v∥ = s Ωce/γ picks out electrons that drive a direct maser near s Ωce.

Narrative
1
A loss cone, horseshoe, or ring creates free energy in the perpendicular part of velocity space.
2
Only electrons on the resonance curve ω − k∥ v∥ = s Ωce/γ can exchange energy efficiently with the wave.
3
αlc shapes the velocity-space source; θbeam is the emitted beam angle. The resonance patch links them.
4
Growth survives only when the anisotropy is strong enough and ωpe/Ωce stays small.
