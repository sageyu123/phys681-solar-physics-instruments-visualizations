Three Frequency Conversions, One Tuned RF Slice

This page turns the EOVSA downconversion chain into a visual story. Adjust the RF tuning slot, then watch the same spectral slice move through the tunable first mixer, the fixed second LO, and the digitizer aliasing step.

The key idea is not just that frequencies move. The ordering flips each time a mirror conversion happens. After one mirror the RF order is inverted, after two it is restored, and after the third it is inverted again by the 1200 MHz digitizer clock.

0. Mixer Math

A mixer multiplies two sinusoids: RF at frequency fRF and local oscillator at fLO. The product contains no energy at the original frequencies — only at their sum fLO + fRF and difference |fLO − fRF|. A bandpass filter then selects whichever product is useful. The same colormap (turbo_r) colors the RF band in Stage 1.

1–2. RF to second IF
The RF slice is mirrored twice: once by the tunable LO₁ (inverting), once by the fixed LO₂ (restoring order).

RF TO SECOND IF

Two successive mirror conversions carry the chosen RF slice from the 1–18 GHz band down to the 825–1150 MHz second IF. The first mirror (tunable LO₁) inverts the RF ordering; the second mirror (fixed LO₂ = 21.15 GHz) restores it.
