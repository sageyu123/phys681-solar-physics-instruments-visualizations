What To Notice

  • Uniform illumination narrows the beam but makes sidelobes more obvious.
  • Taper reduces the effective collecting diameter and lowers point-source gain.
  • For extended sources, the beam average across the disk matters just as much as the total flux.
  • Weak calibrators add only a tiny excess above blank sky and instrument noise on small dishes; the Sun does not.

Interpretation

A quiet-Sun disk does not fill the whole beam, so part of Ta is still blank sky. Even so, the source term is large enough to drive the on-source antenna temperature into the kilokelvin regime.
