EOVSA Corner Turn

This interactive demo links one 10.24 µs F-engine frame to the packetized corner turn. The top panel shows a synthetic ADC-like voltage block and the PFB-style window applied before the FFT; the lower diagram shows one active 512-channel slice being redistributed from all 8 ROACH boards to one destination X-engine while the corresponding RF region is highlighted across the 1–18 GHz EOVSA tuning range.

F-Engine Time Block

One 10.24 µs frame and the 1792-frame accumulation to one 18.35 ms band dwell.

Frame duration matches EOVSA documentation: 8192 samples × 1.25 ns/sample = 10.24 µs. Below the waveform, the panel expands that single frame into the per-band cadence: 1792 × 10.24 µs = 18.35 ms on one band.

RF Spectrum Context

The full axis spans 1–18 GHz. The darker window is the current Ck slice inside the active 325 MHz band.

Corner Turn Data Flow

The active slice Ck is regrouped from all 8 source boards onto destination ROACH k.

Only the current 8 active transfers are drawn at full strength so the board-level corner turn remains readable.
