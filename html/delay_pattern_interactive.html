EOVSA Delay Explorer: Multiband Offset vs. Inband Slope

This view separates two related but different EOVSA observables. The multiband delay moves the phase from one tuning band center to the next, while the inband residual delay tilts the phase inside a single band after the coarse delay center has already been set. The key teaching point is that the broad GHz-scale band-center trend and the finer within-band slope are not the same object.

\[\phi(\nu) \approx -2\pi \nu_c\,\tau_{\mathrm{MB}}\;-\;2\pi(\nu-\nu_c)\,\tau_{\mathrm{IB}}\]
Sign convention: positive delay means antenna j lags i, so increasing delay produces a more negative phase slope with increasing frequency.
Model: \(\tau_{\mathrm{MB}}\) is a teaching proxy for the broadband delay-center offset seen across bands; \(\tau_{\mathrm{IB}}\) is the residual fine delay seen as a slope within a single band. 325 MHz = post-2019 grid; 500 MHz = older grid.

Conceptual Bridge Delay-center offset across bands versus residual slope within one band

In a pure-delay system, one delay controls everything. In EOVSA operations it is more useful to separate what you measure: a broad band-center phase progression across many tunings (multiband delay) and a residual linear slope within one selected band (inband delay).

This teaching model uses \(\tau_{\mathrm{MB}}\) to set the phase from one band center to the next, and \(\tau_{\mathrm{IB}}\) to set the slope inside the chosen band after the coarse delay center has already been established.
