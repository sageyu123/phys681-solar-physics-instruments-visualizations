Plasma Emission Explorer

A fast electron beam carves a bump on the tail of the distribution in v∥. The unstable slope drives Langmuir waves near ωpe, a fraction escapes as EM radiation at the fundamental and second harmonic, and the descending frequency draws a Type III lane on the dynamic spectrum.

Narrative
1
A field-aligned beam creates a bump on the tail of f(v∥).
2
Where ∂f/∂v∥ > 0, Langmuir waves grow near the local plasma frequency.
3
Nonlinear conversion lets some of that electrostatic energy escape at fp and 2fp.
4
Because density decreases outward, fp falls with height, so the radio lane drifts downward in ν–t space.
