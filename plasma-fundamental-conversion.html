Fundamental branch

Scattering / Mode Conversion

A primary Langmuir wave cannot escape directly. A low-frequency density or ion-sound fluctuation supplies an opposite-directed wavevector and scatters the energy onto the transverse electromagnetic branch.

How to read this page
1
L starts as an electrostatic Langmuir wave: strong plasma oscillation, poor direct escape.
2
S is a slow density or ion-sound ripple. In this geometry it points opposite to L, changing the wavevector much more than the frequency.
3
The scattered wave lands on the transverse branch as T, so it can propagate as fundamental radio emission near ωpe.
