Radio Interferometry: Delay, Fringes & Bandwidth

Two antennas separated by baseline b observe a source in direction s. The geometric delay τg = b·s/c produces a fringe pattern across the sky. Finite bandwidth Δν limits coherence, producing the delay beam (sinc envelope).

Drag the 3D scene to move the source direction — the fringe response curve (wavy line) lives in the plane containing the baseline and source, and rotates with it. Increase bandwidth to watch the delay beam narrow and the phasor sum shrink.
