Flux Density  Sν vs. Distance

Why Iν is constant
As r doubles, dΩ shrinks 4× but so does the solid angle subtended — the photon surface brightness is preserved exactly at every distance.
The Jansky
Sν = ∫Iν cos θ dΩ  [W m−2 Hz−1]. One Jansky = 10−26 W m−2 Hz−1 — the standard radio flux density unit.
Brightness Temperature
Tb = c2Iν / (2kν2). For a blackbody, Tb = T in the Rayleigh–Jeans regime and, like Iν, it is independent of distance.
